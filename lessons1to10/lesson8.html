Front

Frente

Repeate every sentence 5 time loudly

  • The front door is open.

    A porta da frente está aberta.

  • There is a tree in front of the house.

    Há uma árvore na frente da casa.

  • I sat in the front of the bus.

    Sentei-me na frente do autocarro.

  • The garden is in front of the house.

    O jardim fica na frente da casa.

                            Portuguese: O jardim fica à frente da casa.

                        O: The (masculine singular definite article)
                        jardim: garden (masculine noun)
                        fica: is located (verb)
                        à frente de: in front of (preposition)
                        a: the (feminine singular definite article)
                        casa: house (feminine noun)

  • He was sitting in front of me.

    Ele estava sentado na frente de mim.

Now listen to these sentences and say them five times for practice. Try learning by writing the sentence down.

ਹੁਣ ਵਾਕ ਨੂੰ ਪੰਜ ਵਾਰ ਬੋਲੋ ਤੇ ਲਿਖ ਕੇ ਯਾਦ ਕਰਨ ਦੀ ਕੋਸ਼ਿਸ਼ ਕਰੋ !

अब वाकया को ५ वर बोले और लिख कर याद करने की कोशिश करें !

Back

Trás

Repeate every sentence 5 time loudly

  • I went back.

    Voltei para trás.

                          I: Eu
                      turned: voltei
                      back: para trás
                      Breakdown:

                      Eu: This is the subject pronoun 
                      in Portuguese, meaning "I".

                      Voltei: This is the past tense of the 
                      verb "voltar", which means "to return" or "to turn back".

                      para trás: This prepositional 
                      phrase means "backwards" or "back."

                      Therefore, "Voltei para trás" accurately 
                      translates "I turned back" in Portuguese

  • I left the backpack on my back.

    Deixei a mochila às minhas costas.

                            I: Eu
                        left: deixei
                        my: minha
                        backpack: mochila
                        on: nas
                        my: minhas
                        back: costas
                        Portuguese translation: Deixei a minha 
                        mochila nas minhas costas.

  • He went back.

    Ele voltou para trás.

  • Take a step back.

    Dê um passo atrás.

                            Dê: This is the imperative form of the verb
                         "dar" (to give), used to give a command or 
                         instruction (you/informal).

                        um: This is the indefinite article for 
                        masculine singular nouns, meaning "a" 
                        or "one."

                        passo: This is the noun "step."
                        atrás: This is an adverb meaning "backwards" 
                        or "behind."

                        Therefore, "Dê um passo atrás" translates to 
                        "Take a step back" in Portuguese.

  • Back pain is very common.

    A dor nas costas é muito comum.

                            Dor: This is the noun "pain."

                        nas costas: This prepositional phrase 
                        translates to "in the back."

                        é: This is the verb "to be" in the 
                        third person singular present indicative.

                        muito: This adverb means "very."

                        comum: This adjective means "common."

                        Therefore, "Dor nas costas é muito comum" 
                        translates to "Back pain is very common" in 
                        Portuguese.

Now listen to these sentences and say them five times for practice. Try learning by writing the sentence down.

ਹੁਣ ਵਾਕ ਨੂੰ ਪੰਜ ਵਾਰ ਬੋਲੋ ਤੇ ਲਿਖ ਕੇ ਯਾਦ ਕਰਨ ਦੀ ਕੋਸ਼ਿਸ਼ ਕਰੋ !

अब वाकया को ५ वर बोले और लिख कर याद करने की कोशिश करें !

Right

Direita

Repeate every sentence 5 time loudly

  • Turn right.

    Vire à direita.

                          Vire: This is the imperative form of the verb 
                      "virar" (to turn), used to give a command or 
                      instruction (you/informal).

                      à direita: This prepositional phrase means 
                      "to the right."

                      Therefore, "Vire à direita" translates to 
                      "Turn right" in Portuguese.

  • He is sitting on my right.

    Está sentado à minha direita.

  • The store is on the right.

    A loja fica à direita.

  • I am right.

    Tenho razão.

                            Tenho: This is the present tense of 
                        the verb "ter" (to have).

                        razão: This noun means "reason" or 
                        "right."

                        Therefore, "Tenho razão" translates 
                        to "I am right" in Portuguese.

  • Write your name in the right corner of the page.

    Escreva o seu nome no canto direito da página.

Now listen to these sentences and say them five times for practice. Try learning by writing the sentence down.

ਹੁਣ ਵਾਕ ਨੂੰ ਪੰਜ ਵਾਰ ਬੋਲੋ ਤੇ ਲਿਖ ਕੇ ਯਾਦ ਕਰਨ ਦੀ ਕੋਸ਼ਿਸ਼ ਕਰੋ !

अब वाकया को ५ वर बोले और लिख कर याद करने की कोशिश करें !

Up

Cima

Repeate every sentence 5 time loudly

  • Look up!

    Olhe para cima!

                        Olhe: This is the imperative form of the verb 
                    "olhar" (to look), used to give a command or 
                    instruction (you/informal).

                    para cima: This prepositional phrase means
                    "upwards" or "up."

                    Therefore, "Olhe para cima!" translates to 
                    "Look up!" in Portuguese.

  • He went up the stairs.

    Subiu as escadas.

                          Ele: This is the subject pronoun in Portuguese, 
                      meaning "He."

                      subiu: This is the past tense of the verb "subir,"
                      which means "to go up" or "to climb."

                      as: This is the definite article for feminine 
                      plural nouns, meaning "the."

                      escadas: This is the plural noun for "stairs."

                      Therefore, "Ele subiu as escadas" translates 
                      to "He went up the stairs" in Portuguese.

  • The plane is going up.

    O avião está a subir. (está a subir: is going up (gerund))

  • Stand up from the chair.

    Levante-se da cadeira. ( Stand up (imperative form of "levantar-se" - reflexive verb) )

                          Levante-se: This is the reflexive imperative form
                       of the verb "levantar" (to raise, to lift), meaning 
                       "stand up." The reflexive pronoun "-se" is attached 
                       to the verb to indicate that the action is performed 
                       by the subject on themselves.

                      da: This is the preposition "de" (from) contracted with 
                      the definite article "a" (feminine singular), meaning "from the."

                      cadeira: This is the noun "chair" (feminine).

                      Therefore, "Levante-se da cadeira" translates to "Stand up from the 
                      chair" in Portuguese.

  • The price of bread went up.

    O preço do pão subiu.

                          O: The (masculine singular definite article)
                      preço: price (masculine noun)
                      do: of the (masculine singular)
                      pão: bread (masculine noun)
                      subiu: increased (past tense of the verb "subir")

Now listen to these sentences and say them five times for practice. Try learning by writing the sentence down.

ਹੁਣ ਵਾਕ ਨੂੰ ਪੰਜ ਵਾਰ ਬੋਲੋ ਤੇ ਲਿਖ ਕੇ ਯਾਦ ਕਰਨ ਦੀ ਕੋਸ਼ਿਸ਼ ਕਰੋ !

अब वाकया को ५ वर बोले और लिख कर याद करने की कोशिश करें !

Down

Baixo

Repeate every sentence 5 time loudly

  • He went down the stairs.

    Desceu as escadas. ( Desceu: He went down (past tense of "descer") )

                        Ele: This is the subject pronoun 
                    in Portuguese, meaning "He."

                    desceu: This is the past tense of 
                    the verb "descer," which means "to go down."

                    as: This is the definite article for feminine
                    plural nouns, meaning "the."

                    escadas: This is the plural noun for "stairs."

                    Therefore, "Ele desceu as escadas" translates 
                    to "He went down the stairs" in Portuguese.

  • Look down.

    Olhe para baixo.

  • The plane is going down.

    O avião está a descer. ( está a descer: is going down (gerund) )

  • Sit down.

    Sente-se: Sit down (imperative form of "sentar-se" - reflexive verb)

  • The price of sugar went down.

    O preço do açúcar baixou.

                            O: The (masculine singular definite article)
                        preço: price (masculine noun)
                        do: of the (masculine singular)
                        açúcar: sugar (masculine noun)
                        baixou: decreased (past tense of the verb "baixar")
                        Explanation:
                        O preço: This is the subject of the sentence, meaning 
                        "the price."

                        do açúcar: This is a prepositional phrase specifying 
                        whose price it is – "of sugar."

                        baixou: This is the verb, indicating that the price 
                        experienced a decrease.

                        Translation: The price of sugar decreased.

                        Why "baixou" is better than "caiu" in this context:

                        Baixar: This verb specifically means "to lower" or 
                        "to decrease." It directly conveys the idea of a 
                        reduction.

                        Cair: While "cair" can also mean "to fall" or 
                        "to decrease," it can have a more general connotation 
                        of something dropping or falling. In the context of 
                        prices, "baixar" is more precise and commonly used 
                        to express a decrease.

Now listen to these sentences and say them five times for practice. Try learning by writing the sentence down.

ਹੁਣ ਵਾਕ ਨੂੰ ਪੰਜ ਵਾਰ ਬੋਲੋ ਤੇ ਲਿਖ ਕੇ ਯਾਦ ਕਰਨ ਦੀ ਕੋਸ਼ਿਸ਼ ਕਰੋ !

अब वाकया को ५ वर बोले और लिख कर याद करने की कोशिश करें !
